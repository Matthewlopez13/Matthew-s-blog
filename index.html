Evolution of Computing : Key milestones and influentional figures

    1. Early Foundations
  • (ca. 3000 BC): One of the first tools for arithmetic.
  • John Napier (1617): Invented Napier’s Bones, an early mechanical device for multiplication.
  • Blaise Pascal (1642): Created the Pascaline, a mechanical calculator for addition and subtraction.

    2. 19th Century – The Mechanical Era
  • Charles Babbage (1820s): Designed the Difference Engine and the Analytical Engine, considered the first concepts of a general-purpose computer.
  • Ada Lovelace (1840s): Known as the first computer programmer, she wrote algorithms for Babbage’s Analytical Engine, recognizing its potential beyond math.
    •

    3. 20th Century – The Dawn of Modern Computing
  • Alan Turing (1936): Developed the concept of the Turing Machine, a theoretical foundation for computers and algorithms.
  • Konrad Zuse (1941): Built the Z3, the world’s first programmable digital computer.
  • ENIAC (1945): Built by John Mauchly and J. Presper Eckert, it was the first general-purpose, electronic, programmable computer.
  • John von Neumann (1945): Developed the von Neumann architecture, which introduced stored-program concepts still used in modern computers.
    •

    4. Mid-20th Century – The Rise of Electronics
  • Transistors (1947): Invented by John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley, transistors replaced vacuum tubes and made computers smaller and more efficient.
  • IBM (1950s-60s): Pioneered the development of mainframe computers, widely used by businesses and governments.
    •

    5. 1970s – The Personal Computer Revolution
  • Intel 4004 (1971): The first commercially available microprocessor, developed by Ted Hoff and others, sparked the personal computing revolution.
  • Altair 8800 (1975): Sparked the personal computing era, leading to the founding of Microsoft by Bill Gates and Paul Allen.
  • Apple (1976): Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak created the Apple I, a key moment in the personal computing industry.
  • IBM PC (1981): Standardized personal computers for mass markets.
    •

    6. 1990s – The Internet Era
  • Tim Berners-Lee (1990): Invented the World Wide Web, making the internet accessible through hypertext links and web browsers.
  • Netscape and Internet Browsers (1994): Marc Andreessen’s browser made the web user-friendly and contributed to the internet’s rapid growth.
    •

    7. 21st Century – Mobile Computing and AI
  • iPhone (2007): Launched by Steve Jobs, the iPhone revolutionized mobile computing and paved the way for smartphones.
  • Artificial Intelligence (2010s-present): Geoffrey Hinton, Yann LeCun, and Andrew Ng have made pioneering contributions to AI, deep learning, and neural networks.
  • Quantum Computing (Present): Figures like David Deutsch are exploring the next frontier of computing with quantum mechanics.

    Introduction To IT Basics

    1.Hardware

  • The physical components like computers, servers, and routers

    • 2.Software

  • Programs and applications that run on devices, such as operating systems, word processors, and databases.

    • 3.Networks

  • Systems that connect computers and devices, enabling communication and data sharing, like the internet or local area networks (LAN).

    • 4.Data Management

  • The storage, retrieval, and security of data, ensuring information is accurate and accessible when needed.
